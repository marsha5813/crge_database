China: Context (1950-1960)

Governmental Regulations on Religion and Legal Cases

Regulations Related to Religion

  1. Central Committee of the Communist Party of China on Issues Concerning Catholicism and Christianity, 1950 (Edited by the United Front Work Department of the Central Committee of the Communist Party of China, 1958: 1911-1914)

In August 1950, the Central Committee of the Communist Party of China issued the ‚ÄúInstructions on Issues Concerning Catholicism and Christianity,‚Äù which defined the issues related to Catholicism and Christianity as both a ‚Äúreligious issue‚Äù and a tool used by imperialism to conduct cultural invasion in our country. Regarding the former, the document stated that while Marxists believe religion is ‚Äúharmful to the awaking of the people,‚Äù they oppose ‚Äúrelying solely on administrative orders and simplistic impatient methods‚Äù to address religious issues, because religious issues also have a ‚Äúmass nature‚Äù that requires careful steps; otherwise, it would lead to significant resentment and hostility among the large group of followers toward the CCP. Therefore, the Party‚Äôs policy is to insist on protecting the freedom of belief while also conducting patriotic propaganda among the faithful within Christianity and Catholicism to ‚Äúexpose the imperialist cultural invasion and espionage schemes,‚Äù so that it can ‚Äútransform from tools of imperialism into a religious cause of the Chinese people.‚Äù The ‚ÄúInstructions‚Äù placed special emphasis on the role of ‚Äúpatriotic believers,‚Äù who must be ‚Äúled and supported‚Äù to ‚Äúunite‚Äù the majority of devout believers and ‚Äúoppose‚Äù the few reactionaries still colluding with imperialism. It is worth noting that the ‚ÄúInstructions‚Äù emphasize opposition to imperialism does not equate to ‚Äúconducting a mass anti-religion movement,‚Äù reiterating that ‚Äúimplementing the common program, realizing land reform, striving for fundamental improvements in finance and economy, and widely promoting materialism and scientific knowledge will gradually reduce the space for religion.‚Äù This is the first document after the establishment of the PRC that elaborated on its policies regarding Christianity and Catholicism, reflecting the tone of ‚ÄúNew Democracy,‚Äù limiting struggles to ‚Äúexposing imperialist cultural invasion and conspiracy activities,‚Äù ‚Äúopposing the few reactionaries still colluding with imperialism,‚Äù and ‚Äúfreeing the church from the influence of imperialism and its economic relations,‚Äù also reaffirming that related struggles ‚Äúshould not implicate the entire church, church buildings, or church schools,‚Äù and must ensure ‚Äúthe majority of believers also unite under the banner of patriotism.‚Äù

  1. Central Committee of the Communist Party of China on Handling Procedures for Cultural and Educational Relief Organizations and Religious Groups Receiving US Subsidies, 1950 (Edited by the United Front Work Department of the Central Committee of the Communist Party of China, 1958: 1923-1925)

With the military confrontation between China and the US on the Korean Peninsula, on December 16, the United States announced the implementation of an economic embargo against China and froze Chinese public and private properties in the US. On December 28, the Chinese government also ordered the regulation of American properties in China and froze American public and private deposits in China, issuing the ‚ÄúInstructions on Handling Procedures for Cultural and Educational Relief Organizations and Religious Groups Receiving US Subsidies,‚Äù which stated that due to long-term imperialist aggression, many cultural and educational relief organizations and religious groups in China received US subsidies. Now, ‚Äúas the anti-American patriotic movement has reached a climax,‚Äù coupled with ‚Äúthe American imperialists freezing our funds,‚Äù it was decided to adopt registration and takeover methods, ‚Äúto completely sever their ties with American imperialism.‚Äù The State Council of the Central People‚Äôs Government will announce related registration methods, requiring these organizations to register. The instructions also proposed three handling methods for takeovers: (1) Government takeover as state agencies: mainly targeting some schools, hospitals, and all relief organizations; (2) Commissioning Chinese private groups to run them, with state subsidies: mainly targeting most universities, primary and secondary schools, and some hospitals; (3) Implementing self-management by Chinese people: mainly targeting some economically self-sufficient primary schools and all religious organizations. The instructions also stated: ‚ÄúIt is necessary to launch a mass anti-American patriotic movement in all cultural and educational relief organizations and religious groups receiving US subsidies, especially in church schools, to expose imperialist invasion conspiracies‚Ä¶ to hold accusations and demonstrations against American imperialism, expose and combat the few reactionaries, and unite the vast majority of the masses.‚Äù This is the most important step to ensure that these organizations ‚Äúforever sever ties with imperialist influence.‚Äù During the process, ‚Äúit is crucial to strictly ensure no anti-religion or exclusion of foreigners,‚Äù and that if foreigners sincerely ‚Äúserve our country with their knowledge and skills and comply with the laws of the Chinese government, they may be retained depending on the situation.‚Äù

On December 29, the 65th executive meeting of the State Council approved the ‚ÄúDecision of the State Council of the Central People‚Äôs Government on Handling the Policies for Cultural and Educational Relief Organizations and Religious Groups Receiving US Subsidies,‚Äù and the ‚ÄúRegulations on the Registration of Cultural and Educational Relief Organizations and Religious Groups Receiving Foreign Subsidies and Foreign Investments.‚Äù On January 14, 1951, the Cultural and Educational Committee issued the ‚ÄúImplementation Measures for the Registration of Cultural and Educational Relief Organizations and Religious Groups Receiving Foreign Subsidies and Foreign Investments.‚Äù

  1. Central Committee of the Communist Party of China on the Establishment of a Religious Issues Committee and Religious Affairs Office, 1951 (Edited by the United Front Work Department of the Central Committee of the Communist Party of China, 1958: 1927-1928)

On January 9, 1951, the Central Committee of the Communist Party of China issued ‚ÄúInstructions on the Establishment of a Religious Issues Committee and Religious Affairs Office,‚Äù stating that ‚Äúin order to systematically eliminate the influence of imperialism in China, to combat imperialism‚Äôs conspiracy to strengthen its influence on the Chinese people through religion (Catholicism, Christianity, and Buddhism), and to promote the anti-imperialist patriotic movement and self-governance, self-financing, and self-propagation among religious groups, the decision of the State Council regarding the handling of cultural and educational relief organizations and religious groups receiving US subsidies must be implemented, and specialized departments for handling religious affairs must be established within the Party and government.‚Äù Its responsibilities include fully understanding the situations of various religions, strengthening communication with religious figures, assisting the Party and government in determining policies on religious issues, and addressing specific matters related to religion. Accordingly, the instructions decided: (1) On the Party organizational side, a ‚ÄúReligious Issues Committee‚Äù must be organized under the Central Committee and various central bureaus and branches, composed of responsible Party members from the propaganda department, United Front Work Department, Youth League, and governmental agencies such as public security, foreign affairs, internal affairs, intelligence, ethnic affairs, and relief, to be led by the propaganda department, initially guiding and researching policies and guidelines on the issues of Christianity, Catholicism, and Buddhism, and designating specific individuals to handle daily work; (2) On the government side, a ‚ÄúReligious Affairs Office‚Äù will be established under the Cultural and Educational Committee of the State Council and the Cultural and Educational Committees of the People‚Äôs Governments in major administrative regions (Military and Political Committees) to handle and research policy work related to Christianity, Catholicism, and Buddhism. As for work involving Islam and Lamaism, it will be the responsibility of the Ethnic Affairs Commission, with the Religious Affairs Office providing assistance.

  1. Central Committee of the Communist Party of China on Actively Promoting the Religious Reform Movement Instructions and Re-Instructions, 1951 (Edited by the United Front Work Department of the Central Committee of the Communist Party of China, 1958: 1929-1932, 1936-1937)

The First National Religious Work Conference was held in Beijing from March 15 to 19, 1951. Before the conference, on March 5, the Central Committee issued ‚ÄúInstructions on Actively Promoting the Religious Reform Movement,‚Äù categorizing the reform movements in Christianity and Catholicism as an important part of the ‚Äúanti-imperialist political struggle,‚Äù with the primary objective of eliminating the influence of imperialism on China‚Äôs cultural invasion. To successfully advance the reform movement, the government must act proactively and negotiate with upper-level Chinese leaders, while under lower-level believers carry out patriotic education, ‚Äúmobilizing them to pressure the upper levels to implement self-governance, self-financing, and self-propagation.‚Äù The ‚ÄúInstructions‚Äù clearly stated that when promoting the religious reform movement in various places, ‚Äúit must be pointed out that the churches in China have been used by imperialism as tools to invade China and undermine the new China,‚Äù while distinguishing ‚Äúordinary believers from the few reactionaries among believers‚Äù to ‚Äúwin over the majority.‚Äù At the same time, it should emphasize that ‚Äúgood believers must be good patriotic citizens.‚Äù Education for believers should focus on patriotism, ‚Äúavoiding discussions of materialism or idealism, and not harming the religious feelings of believers.‚Äù It further stated: ‚ÄúIn this movement, we must unite the vast majority of the religious community, organize, develop, and consolidate a united front against imperialism throughout the national religious circles,‚Äù while patiently ‚Äúinspiring their patriotism, alleviating their concerns, and avoiding impatience, which could lead to oppression against religion and exclusion.‚Äù Regarding law-abiding foreign missionaries who have been in China for many years, as long as they ‚Äúdo not engage in any political activities and do not manage church affairs, they may continue to stay.‚Äù As for the Vatican, under the conditions of ‚Äúnon-interference in China‚Äôs religious reform movement and non-political activity in China, Chinese believers and the Vatican may still have purely religious ties.‚Äù The instructions indicated that a meeting would be held in April for Christianity to discuss further realization of the ‚ÄúThree Selfs,‚Äù and it is hoped that a Catholic meeting could also be convened in the latter half of the year.

In April, the Central Committee issued further ‚ÄúInstructions on Actively Leading the Religious Reform Movement,‚Äù indicating that in areas where religious activities have been absent for many years, ‚Äúreligious activities should no longer be resumed, nor should any religious reform movement be conducted, and no outside missionaries or reorganization of church affairs should be allowed,‚Äù because the fundamental principle regarding religious activities is ‚Äúto transform rather than develop or prohibit.‚Äù At the same time, those ‚Äúremaining faithful believers‚Äù should be regarded as an important target of the united front, guiding them to ‚Äúcompletely separate politically from the reactionaries of imperialism and be led by us,‚Äù directly publicizing the anti-American and anti-imperialist patriotic campaign, promoting the religious policies of the People‚Äôs Government, exposing the crimes of imperialism utilizing Catholicism and Christianity for invasion, and encouraging them to participate in learning and accusations, thus attracting them into the popular democratic united front.

  1. Central Committee of the Communist Party of China on Handling the Relationship Between Chinese Catholics and the Vatican, 1951 (Edited by the United Front Work Department of the Central Committee of the Communist Party of China, 1958: 1934-1935)

On March 7, 1951, the Central Committee of the Communist Party of China issued ‚ÄúInstructions on Handling the Relationship Between Chinese Catholics and the Vatican,‚Äù stating that the Vatican is one of the centers of imperialist anti-communist conspiracies. Therefore, the principle of reform for Catholicism must be to sever ties with the Vatican in order to be truly realized. However, most Chinese bishops, priests, and believers have not yet recognized the necessity of breaking away from the Vatican, and resolving this issue will still require considerable time. Thus, in order to win over the ‚Äúintermediate forces‚Äù within Catholicism, it is appropriate to clarify that ‚Äúit is not prohibited for Chinese Catholics to maintain purely religious ties with the Vatican, but we firmly oppose any actions or statements by the Vatican that are against the democratic cause of the Chinese people and suppress or undermine the implementation of true self-reliant reforms within Chinese Catholicism.‚Äù Hence, it is suggested that in the future, when Catholic leaders issue statements, it is best to not mention the Vatican (or the Pope); if mentioned, it should declare that the Vatican must not interfere in Chinese politics and must not conduct anti-People‚Äôs Government, anti-communist, anti-patriotic, or anti-reform propaganda and activities.

  1. Central Committee of the Communist Party of China on Issues Concerning Buddhism Among the Han Nationality, 1951 (Edited by the United Front Work Department of the Central Committee of the Communist Party of China, 1958: 1940-1942)

On June 12, 1951, the Central Committee of the Communist Party of China issued ‚ÄúInstructions on Issues Concerning Buddhism Among the Han Nationality,‚Äù stating that the relationship between Buddhism and imperialism is less pronounced than that of Catholicism and Christianity, but imperialism continues to employ Buddhism as a tool for aggression and enslaving people in Southeast Asia. In China, Buddhism has long become a tool for the feudal ruling class to oppress and exploit peasants, and it still holds significant influence among the Han people. Buddhism among the Han Nationality, unlike the Lamaist issues related to ethnic problems, can be treated as a ‚Äúpurely religious issue.‚Äù Currently, some regions have monks and laypersons participating in patriotic demonstration movements; the most important task for Buddhism is to eliminate spies, agents, and other reactionaries, thus purging imperialist political influence among Buddhists. Ordinary practitioners who merely offer incense and worship should not be viewed as Buddhists. The fundamental principles for Buddhist work include: (1) Monks and nuns should not be left to their own devices, and not be simplified or rushed into action; they must be transformed through labor and patriotic movements, but mobilization for secularization should not be rushed; (2) When the government requisitions or borrows temple properties, prior negotiation should take place, and no occupation of Buddhist halls or shrine rooms should occur, with proper protection for temples with historical or cultural value; (3) Local Buddhist organizations should register according to the regulations for social organizations, and those without revolutionary activities may exist legally; (4) Reforms in Buddhism must be proposed and carried out by the Buddhists themselves, with necessary support from the government; (5) Active members among Buddhists should be cultivated; (6) Investigation and research on the situation of Buddhism should be strengthened in various places; (7) Taoism can refer to the policy guidelines of Buddhism.

On September 20, 1954, the First Session of the First National People‚Äôs Congress passed the ‚ÄúConstitution of the People‚Äôs Republic of China,‚Äù which states in Article 8: ‚ÄúCitizens of the People‚Äôs Republic of China enjoy freedom of religious belief.‚Äù

  1. Central Committee‚Äôs Approval of the ‚ÄúReport on the Leadership Relationship, Organizational Structure, and Business Scope of the Religious Affairs Bureau of the State Council‚Äù 1955 (Edited by the United Front Work Department of the Central Committee of the Communist Party of China, 1958: 2054-2055)

On October 18, 1955, the Central Committee approved the report from the Party Group of the Religious Affairs Bureau of the State Council regarding the leadership relationship, organizational structure, and business scope of religious work. According to the report, the Party Group of the Religious Affairs Bureau of the State Council believed that the leadership of religious work within the Party and government showed inconsistencies, which was detrimental to implementing the Party‚Äôs religious policies, and recommended the following adjustments: (1) Within the government, Han nationality Buddhism and Taoism will be reallocated under the management of all levels of religious affairs departments; (2) Religious work within the Party should be unified under the leadership of Party committees at all levels, with policies concerning the United Front handled by the United Front Work Department and publicity policies by the Propaganda Department; issues relating to the religious problems of ethnic minorities should be jointly handled by local religious affairs departments and ethnic affairs departments at all levels; (3) Each city Party committee and autonomous region Party committee shall establish religious work committees composed of leaders from the Party‚Äôs propaganda department, United Front Work Department, and government people‚Äôs committees, public security, religious affairs offices, Youth League, and Women‚Äôs Federation, directly responsible to the Party committee. Each level of the Party committee should appoint a standing committee member responsible for religious work, and the government people‚Äôs committee should also designate a Party member to lead the religious work.

  1. Central Committee of the Communist Party of China on the Issue of Religious Work‚Äôs Party Affiliation, 1957 (Edited by the United Front Work Department of the Central Committee of the Communist Party of China, 1958: 2078)

On March 14, 1957, the Central Committee of the Communist Party of China issued a notice regarding the issues of religious work‚Äôs affiliation within the Party, deciding that the work related to Catholicism, Christianity, Buddhism, Taoism, and Islam would be uniformly overseen by the United Front departments at all levels within the Party and managed by religious affairs departments at all levels within the government.

  1. The Suppression of the Yiguan Dao (Li Wanqu 1991: 241-275; Wang Qinduan 2022: 43-52)
