1894 Pyongyang Persecution

An instance of persecution in Pyongyang in 1894, shortly before the Donghak Rebellion, provides a good case in point of anti-foreign and anti-Christian atmosphere. At that time, Pyongyang was not a treaty city and foreign residence was not permitted there, much less the Christian religion. Yet, both the Presbyterian and Methodist missionaries had visited this city and made several converts there. Their justification for their activities was that the Japanese were already there and that Americans should deserve the same rights of residence as citizens of a most-favored-nation. Moreover, they purchased property in the name of local converts and made it into a clandestine church. However, the illegal Christian intrusion was soon detected by local residents and reported to the local authorities. The governor of Pyongyang quickly arrested and jailed the native Christians who had helped the foreigners, while the missionaries were saved from the same fate due to their extraterritorial status. An instance of persecution in Pyongyang in 1894, shortly before the Donghak Rebellion, provides a good case in point of anti-foreign and anti-Christian atmosphere.
