The focus will be on three pro-Christian movements in Japan before 1900. The first movement was the revival that occurred in 1883-84. It means the fourth suppression campaign against hidden Christians (Kakure Kirisitan) in the Urakami area refers to a large-scale attack on Christians in Nagasaki Prefecture from the end of the Edo period to the early Meiji period. Although there had been several previous repressions against hidden Christians in Nagasaki, their presence was revealed in July 1867 when Christians in Urakami refused to accept Buddhist-style funerals. The second incident is the Takahashi Church Incident in 1884. This movement was a persecution by residents against Takahashi Church in Takahashi City, Okayama Prefecture. Okayama Prefecture was originally a strongly anti-Christian area, but in 1882, Matsumura Kaiseki, influenced by James Hamilton Ballagh in Yokohama, was became as a pastor.
